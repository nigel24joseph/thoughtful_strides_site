Nigel Joseph

Counsellor and Psychotherapist — Narre Warren & Telehealth

With almost 20 years' experience, my approach to therapy is grounded in an understanding of how the brain and nervous system respond to stress, life experiences, and relationships. These responses often shape patterns in how we think, feel, and relate to others. Therapy focuses on bringing awareness to these patterns and supporting the development of new ways of responding that feel more helpful and aligned with your goals.

I take an integrative approach, drawing on a range of evidence-based therapies including Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT), Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT), Schema Therapy, Motivational Interviewing, Eye Movement Desensitisation and Reprocessing (EMDR), Radical Exposure Tapping, Solution Focussed Therapy and Positive Psychology. This allows the work to be adapted to your individual circumstances, rather than applying a one-size-fits-all approach.

When working with individuals, the focus is on understanding personal patterns, building insight, and developing practical strategies to support change in day-to-day life. Sessions provide space to reflect, process experiences, and strengthen your ability to respond differently to challenges.

When working with couples, the focus shifts to understanding the patterns that shape communication, connection, and conflict within the relationship. Therapy supports both people to feel heard, gain insight into each other's experiences, and develop new ways of communicating and relating that strengthen connection and stability.

Across both individual and couples work, therapy is collaborative, practical, and paced to suit you. The aim is to support meaningful and lasting change by combining insight with strategies that can be applied in everyday life.
