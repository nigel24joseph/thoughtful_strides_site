Trauma Therapy & Counselling in Narre Warren

Trauma can affect people in many different ways. Sometimes it shows up as flashbacks, nightmares, or feeling on edge. Other times it's a persistent sense of numbness, disconnection, or difficulty trusting others. You may not always recognise your experiences as trauma — but if something from your past is affecting how you feel and function today, therapy can help.

  • Flashbacks, intrusive memories, or distressing dreams
  • Feeling on edge, hypervigilant, or easily startled
  • Emotional numbness, disconnection, or feeling detached from life
  • Difficulty trusting others or feeling safe in relationships
  • Anxiety, depression, or unexplained physical symptoms
  • Shame, guilt, or a persistent sense that something is wrong with you
  • Avoiding people, places, or situations that trigger distressing feelings
  • Complex or childhood trauma affecting day-to-day life

Trauma therapy provides a safe, structured space to process difficult experiences at a pace that feels right for you. Rather than simply talking about what happened, the focus is on how trauma is held in the body and nervous system, and supporting you to move through it in a way that leads to genuine relief and lasting change.

A range of evidence-based approaches are used depending on your needs, including Eye Movement Desensitisation and Reprocessing (EMDR), Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT), Schema Therapy, and Radical Exposure Tapping (RET). These therapies are specifically designed to help the brain process and integrate traumatic memories, reducing their intensity and impact over time.

Whether you have experienced a single traumatic event, ongoing or repeated trauma, or carry experiences from childhood that continue to shape your life today, therapy is tailored to your individual circumstances. The aim is not to erase the past, but to change your relationship with it — so it no longer holds the same power over how you feel and live.

Sessions are collaborative, compassionate, and paced to suit you. You will never be pushed beyond what feels manageable. The goal is to support you to feel safer, more grounded, and more connected to the life you want to live.
