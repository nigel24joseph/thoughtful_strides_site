Support for Individuals

Compassionate support for life's challenges.

People come to counselling for many different reasons. Often, it's a sense that something isn't quite working or feeling the way it used to. This might include:

  • Feeling overwhelmed, stuck, or unsure how to move forward
  • Ongoing stress or difficulty coping with day-to-day life
  • Low mood, lack of motivation, or loss of direction
  • Struggles with confidence, self-worth, or decision-making
  • Navigating life changes such as separation, career shifts, or loss
  • Patterns in relationships that feel difficult or repetitive
  • Wanting to better understand thoughts, emotions, and behaviours
  • A desire for personal growth and greater clarity

Working one-on-one provides a space where you can talk openly about what's happening in your life, at a pace that feels right for you. My role is to listen carefully, understand your experience, and support you to make sense of what you're going through.

Together, we explore patterns in thoughts, emotions, and responses, and how these may have developed over time. This helps to bring greater clarity and understanding, so you can begin to respond in ways that feel more helpful and aligned with what matters to you.

I take a practical and collaborative approach, focusing on what is most relevant for you. This may include developing new ways of coping, improving how you relate to yourself and others, and working toward meaningful changes in your day-to-day life.

Sessions are tailored to your individual needs, drawing on a range of evidence-based approaches. The aim is to support you to feel more grounded, clear, and able to move forward with greater confidence and direction.
